Workshop on Standardised Brain Computer Interface

UTS Tech Festival: How to Bridge the gap between Neuroscience & Computational Intelligence for BCI with Google TensorFlow ?

Wed 22nd Jun 2022, 9:30 am - 3:30 pm AEST

Event description

We are pleased to invite you to the hands-on workshop on how to bridge the gap between Neuroscience and Computational Intelligence to design and develop Brain-Computer Interface. This workshop intends to promote standardization of terminologies and technologies for Brain-Computer Interfaces to computational intelligence practitioners and Neuroscience communities. The workshop promotes the utilization of open-source tools such as Google TensorFlow for enhancing community cohesion and BCI standardization.

The details of the event are as follows:

What:

  • Lots of Brain Computer Interface, Code, Models and Hand-on demonstrations with Neurophyiological devices

  • Coffee, Snacks and Lunch

  • Talks from experts including Google TensorFlow and IEEE Standards

When:

Wednesday, June 22th 2022 from 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM

Where:

UTS (exact location will be posted soon)

Who:

  • BCI Researchers of all stages (graduate, Ph.D., postdocs, faculties) from the Neuroscience and Computational Intelligence community

  • Industry Partners

  • Research Partners

  • All UTS students

  • Anyone else who want to learn

Workshop Outline (tentative)

  1. BCI Introduction: An overview of BCI’s terminologies such as invasive/non-invasive/types of BCI and associated acquisition methods such as EEG/fNIRS/iEEG/implants for multidisciplinary stakeholders

  2. Overview on BCI model and its applications: Demonstrating a functional model for BCI and how it is translated to industrial applications such as neural-prosthetics, entertainment, and monitoring

    • Case studies: Show different examples of both academic and industry works to illustrate the differences of their description

  3. BCI Standards: Interactive Session to develop data standards for BCI utilization. Such as what to store in data both from neuroscience and computational intelligence to fill the gaps between them?

    • Meta-data for EEG related information

    • Meta-data for EEG processing related information

    • Meta-data for ML related information

    • Meta-data for experiment related information

  4. TensorFlow tutorial/crash course from Google

  5. BCI Tutorials using TensorFlow, conducted on Colab: Including all-important principles such as data acquisition and signal processing using, feature selection and extraction, machine learning methods to learn and classify data, and how to use TensorFlow APIs to automate the task for both analysis and classification with following most common BCI paradigms:

    • Event-Related Potential (P300)

    • Steady-state visually evoked potentials (SSVEP)

    • Motor Imagery (MI)

(for hands-on setup, please bring your own laptop)

Recommended background

  • Basic knowledge about BCI and machine learning

  • Basic knowledge in programming
